The 7-Factor Compounder Model

These factors focus on three themes:

Quality

Growth

Market Confirmation

Many successful companies such as NVIDIA, Shopify, and Adobe exhibited most of these characteristics during their growth phases.

1️⃣ Sustained Revenue Growth

Look for companies growing revenue consistently over several years.

Typical thresholds:

3-year revenue CAGR > 20%
5-year revenue CAGR > 15%

This indicates market demand and share expansion.

2️⃣ High Gross Margins

High margins often indicate:

strong pricing power

scalable business models

valuable intellectual property

Guidelines:

Gross margin > 60% (software)
Gross margin > 50% (tech / semiconductors)
3️⃣ Expanding Operating Margins

Operating margins increasing over time suggest operating leverage.

Metric:

Operating margin trend (3-year slope)

Positive slope = improving profitability as revenue grows.

4️⃣ High Return on Invested Capital (ROIC)

This measures how efficiently a company converts capital into profit.

Strong compounders often show:

ROIC > 15%

Companies like Microsoft and Apple consistently maintain high ROIC.

5️⃣ Positive Free Cash Flow Growth

Cash flow growth confirms that earnings are real.

Signal:

FCF growth > revenue growth

When this happens, the business becomes increasingly self-funding.

6️⃣ Earnings Revision Momentum

Stocks often move when analysts increase future earnings estimates.

Track:

EPS estimate changes (last 90 days)

More upward revisions usually correlate with positive price momentum.

Example companies where revisions drove major moves include Tesla and Eli Lilly.

7️⃣ Price Momentum Confirmation

Market confirmation helps avoid buying fundamentally strong companies stuck in long downtrends.

Useful signals:

Price above MA200
Positive 6-month momentum
MACD positive

Momentum signals often reinforce the fundamental story.

Example Scoring Structure

Each factor can be normalized between 0 and 1, then averaged.

Example:

CompounderScore =
0.18 * RevenueGrowth
+ 0.14 * GrossMargin
+ 0.14 * OperatingLeverage
+ 0.14 * ROIC
+ 0.14 * FCFGrowth
+ 0.13 * EPSRevisions
+ 0.13 * PriceMomentum

This produces a score:

0 – 1

Higher values indicate stronger compounder characteristics.
