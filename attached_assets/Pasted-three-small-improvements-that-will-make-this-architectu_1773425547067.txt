Power Law Reality of Stock Markets

Returns in equity markets follow a power-law distribution.

That means:

Most stocks → mediocre
Some stocks → moderate winners
Very few stocks → massive winners

Example distribution (typical over 10–20 years):

Outcome	% of Stocks
Lose money	~40%
Small return	~40%
2–5× winners	~15%
10×+ winners	~5%

Those 5% generate most market wealth.

This pattern is why companies like:

Amazon

NVIDIA

Tesla

Apple

drive huge portions of index returns.

Even professional investors often underestimate this effect.

What Most Quant Systems Do (Incorrect)

Typical systems optimize for:

Best Sharpe ratio
Lowest volatility
Highest average return

But that reduces exposure to extreme winners.

Your model might end up selecting:

10 good stocks

What Venture Investors Do Instead

Firms like Sequoia Capital and Andreessen Horowitz assume:

Most investments will fail.
One or two will drive the entire portfolio.

So they design portfolios that maximize exposure to outliers.
