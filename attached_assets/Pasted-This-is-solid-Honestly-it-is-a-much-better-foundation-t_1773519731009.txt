What I would change in your scoring design
A) Slightly rebalance the top-level weights

Your proposal:

Quality 55%

Opportunity 45%

That is close, but I’d make it a little more quality-led:

Quality Score contribution: 60%

Opportunity Score contribution: 40%

Reason: you do not want short-term tape action overpowering a truly strong business too often.

So:

base = qualityScore * 0.60 + opportunityScore * 0.40

B) Rebalance Company Quality internals slightly

Your proposed:

Quality 30

Growth 25

Balance Sheet 20

Cash Flow 15

CapEfficiency 10

I’d go:

Quality 30

Growth 20

Balance Sheet 20

Cash Flow 20

Capital Efficiency 10

Why: cash flow quality deserves equal footing with balance sheet. It is one of the best anti-fraud / anti-hype filters in the whole stack.

C) Rebalance Opportunity if Sentiment is weak

Your proposed:

Valuation 40

Momentum 35

Sentiment 25

Good if sentiment is real. But until then:

when Sentiment unavailable, redistribute to:

Valuation 55

Momentum 45

Do not let a fake bucket keep a fixed weight.

Verdict logic: mostly yes, with one important cleanup

Your proposed verdict layer is much cleaner than the current setup. I’d support it with one adjustment:

Your current proposed rules overlap a bit awkwardly:

ADD = base ≥ 0.60, OR quality high but opportunity weak

TRIM = quality ≥ 0.65 but opportunity ≤ 0.35

That is directionally right, but I’d make it more deterministic and ordered.

A cleaner rule set:

SELL

base < 0.40

or distress gate triggered

TRIM

qualityScore >= 0.65 && opportunityScore <= 0.35

or base between 0.40 and 0.50 with stretched valuation + poor momentum

HOLD

base >= 0.50

or any hard gate triggered that blocks higher verdicts

ADD

base >= 0.60

or qualityScore >= 0.70 && opportunityScore between 0.35 and 0.52

BUY

base >= 0.70 && qualityScore >= 0.62 && opportunityScore >= 0.52

no hard gates

STRONG BUY

base >= 0.78 && qualityScore >= 0.72 && opportunityScore >= 0.65

no hard gates

coverage adequate

This preserves your idea while reducing ambiguity.

One more thing I’d definitely add: score confidence

This should ship with the refactor.

Not just data coverage, but score confidence:

High confidence = broad factor coverage and no major proxy substitutions

Medium confidence = partial coverage or some proxy use

Low confidence = YF-only, many nulls, missing balance sheet/cash flow depth

Because a BUY with low confidence should feel different from a BUY with full evidence.
