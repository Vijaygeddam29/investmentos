Yeah — this is the bigger credibility problem than the weird ROIC outliers.

You do not just have “some noisy factors.” You have a factor architecture that can produce contradictory suggestions for structural reasons:

Hidden overweighting from duplicate metrics
The same business attribute is being counted multiple times under different family names. That means the displayed family weights are not the real weights. So a user thinks they are seeing diversified evidence, but they are often seeing the same evidence wearing three hats.

At least one true direction bug
workingCapitalDrift scoring opposite ways in two families is not philosophical disagreement — it is a logic bug. That should be fixed immediately.

Mislabelled families
“Sentiment” and parts of “Innovation” are basically pretending to use signals that do not exist in the DB. That is how users end up feeling gaslit by the UI: the label says one thing, the score is actually driven by something else.

No bridge between factor detail and final recommendation
The system can show lots of green sub-metrics and still land on HOLD/ADD/TRIM, but it does not explain which family or entry signal overruled the others. So the recommendation feels arbitrary even when the math is technically consistent.

What I would change from your proposal

I would go a bit further than you did on contradictions:

1. Add factor coverage / confidence

Each family should show something like:

Coverage: 8/10 signals available

Confidence: High / Medium / Low

Because right now a family score built from 3 surviving inputs looks as authoritative as one built from 12. That is sneaky, even if unintended.

2. Separate “missing” from “neutral”

Do not let null signals quietly disappear as though they had no effect. Users should know whether:

the signal was unavailable

the signal was neutral

the signal was weak/strong

That alone will explain a lot of apparent contradictions.

3. Add contribution view

For each family, calculate top positive and negative contributors. Example:

Profitability driven by ROIC and operating margin

Momentum dragged by RSI and distance above MA50

That is more useful than just dumping raw rows.

4. Stop showing the same raw metric twice without context

For fcfYield, if it remains in two places, the UI must explicitly label the role:

Cash Flow Quality: “cash generation relative to price”

Valuation: “cheapness based on cash flow”

But really, cleaner move is to score it once and reference it elsewhere visually, not count it twice.

On the recommendation logic

I would also remove the best engine bonus, yes.

That bonus is exactly the kind of thing that makes users say, “why did this get promoted?” It rewards spikes and makes the recommendation less interpretable. If you want a “don’t miss exceptional companies” feature, do it explicitly as a rule-based override, not via a hidden blend term.

A cleaner structure is:

compute family scores

compute engine scores from family scores

compute regime-weighted composite

apply entry timing as a gate/modifier

produce verdict

produce explanation from dominant positives and negatives

That is legible.
